Ask users for

Biometric residence card or permit details

RECOMMENDED

Ask users for details from their biometric residence card (BRC) or permit (BRP), an identity document that allows users certain rights to live, study and work in the UK.

When to use this pattern

Follow this pattern if this is the identity document users need to:

  • apply for something
  • create, access and recover an account
  • prove their identity
  • update their details

Services using this pattern

  • EU Settlement account recovery
  • Generic Identity Verification
  • Status: right to rent, right to work
  • Update your details

Research

This pattern tested well with a range of users. Research has shown when asked to supply details for the BRC/P, some users began searching online for an example of what this was. Having an image helped users pick the right number and in the right format.

Asking for the date of birth in the next screen made it easier for the user to use the service because it was asking one thing per page. This works well on mobile devices too. The date of birth with the BRC/P number provides the minimal amount of information needed to verify a user’s identity from that document.

Accessibility

Placing the image of the BRC/P inline, directly beneath the question, has made it more accessible with magnification software by keeping the layout left-aligned. We recommend following the guidance on the use of alt text in the GOV.UK Design System.
