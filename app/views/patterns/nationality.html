Country of nationality


Dual nationality

When to use this pattern

Follow this pattern when asking a user for their nationality as part of a service. Only ask this if you need it to deliver a service. Common scenarios when a user may be asked for their nationality:

  • applying for an identity document, such as a passport
  • applying for a travel permit, such as a visa
  • accessing a previous application
  • updating their details

How it works

Make it clear:

  • why you’re asking for a user’s nationality
  • whose nationality you’re asking for, for example, if your user is filling out information about a relative or guardian

Use autocomplete

Use the GOV.UK country and territory autocomplete with this pattern. Depending on the context, allow users to enter:

  • several forms of citizenship. For example, Britain, Scotland or Northern Ireland for a national of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
  • entities that aren’t recognised as countries by the UK, for example Taiwan and Hong Kong

Services using this pattern

  • Apply for the EU Settlement Scheme
  • Generic identity verification
  • Visas and immigration

Research

Further research needs to be done:

  • on the best way to ask for a user’s nationality - for example “What is your country of nationality” or “What is your nationality?”
  • with users who hold multiple nationalities - it can be unclear which nationality they should enter
  • around the scenario with users who are stateless - is there alternative information that users can provide?
  • on when or when not to ask for country of birth - instead of or alongside nationality

We’d also be interested to hear about how services have supported all use cases, for example:

  • country
  • territories
  • location
  • spelling errors
  • other variations in users' answers

Accessibility

If your service uses this pattern, let us know of any insights you have on accessibility considerations.
